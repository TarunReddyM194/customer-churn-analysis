# Business Insights

## Insight 1: Contract Type Has a Strong Impact on Customer Churn

The analysis shows that customers on month-to-month contracts are much more likely to leave compared to customers on long-term contracts. Nearly 43% of 
month-to-month customers churned, while the churn rate for one-year and two-year contracts was significantly lower.

This suggests that customers who commit to longer contracts tend to stay with the company for a longer period.

### Recommendation

The company can focus on converting month-to-month customers into annual or two-year plans by offering discounts, loyalty rewards, or bundled service packages. 
This could help improve customer retention and reduce churn.
